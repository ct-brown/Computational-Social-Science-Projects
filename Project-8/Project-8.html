Introduction

Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the United States, and treating it properly is an important public health goal. However, it is a complex disease with several different risk factors and potential treatments. Physicians typically recommend changes in diet, increased exercise, and/or medication to treat symptoms, but it is difficult to determine how effective any one of these factors is in treating the disease. In this project, you will explore SuperLearner, Targeted Maximum Likelihood Estimation (TMLE), and Longitudinal Targeted Maximum Likelihood Estimation (LTMLE). Using a simulated dataset, you will explore whether taking blood pressure medication reduces mortality risk.

Discussion Questions

# What is a "double robust" estimator? Why does it provide a guarantee of consistency if either the outcome model or propensity score model is correctly specified? Or in other words, why does mispecifying one of the models not break the analysis? \textbf{Hint}: When answering this question, think about how your introductory statistics courses emphasized using theory to determine the correct outcome model, and in this course how we explored the benefits of matching.

# A doubly robust estimator reduces the risk of model misspecification while being efficient (in terms of variance). It provides consistency even when the model might be misspecified because it combines the outcome model and the propensity score model, so if one model is misspecified, the model can still produce consistent estimates if the other model is correctly specified. If the outcome model is misspecified, the model can still recover the true causal effect because of the propensity score model, a benefit of matching.
